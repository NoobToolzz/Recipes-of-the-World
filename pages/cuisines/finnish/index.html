Ruisleipä

Ruisleipä (rye bread) is a dark, sour bread produced extensively in Finland. It is the most popular type of bread in Finland. It is a staple in Finnish cuisine, and holds the status of the national food.

×

Ruisleipä Recipe

Ingredients

  • 3 cups dark rye flour
  • 1 cup whole grain rye flour
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons salt
  • 1 teaspoon caraway seeds (optional)
  • 2 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 1 1/2 cups warm water (110°F/43°C)
  • 1 tablespoon molasses or dark syrup (optional, for sweetness)

Mix Dry Ingredients

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine dark rye flour, whole grain rye flour, salt, and caraway seeds.

Activate Yeast

  1. In a separate bowl, dissolve the active dry yeast in warm water. Allow it to sit for a few minutes until it becomes frothy.

Combine Wet and Dry Ingredients

  1. Pour the activated yeast mixture into the dry ingredients.
  2. Add molasses or dark syrup for a touch of sweetness if desired.

Knead Dough

  1. Mix the ingredients until a dough forms. Turn the dough onto a floured surface and knead for about 10 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic.

First Rise

  1. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover it with a damp cloth, and let it rise in a warm place for 1-2 hours or until it doubles in size.

Shape and Second Rise

  1. Punch down the risen dough, shape it into a loaf, and place it in a greased bread pan.
  2. Cover and let it rise for an additional 30-60 minutes.

Preheat and Bake

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Bake the bread for about 30-40 minutes or until it sounds hollow when tapped on the bottom.

Cool and Slice

  1. Allow the Ruisleipä to cool in the pan for a few minutes before transferring it to a wire rack to cool completely.
  2. Once cooled, slice and enjoy!

This Ruisleipä is delicious with butter, cheese, or traditional Finnish toppings like salmon. Adjust the sweetness and caraway seeds according to your taste preferences.

Poronkäristys

Poronkäristys is the traditional dish of Sámi people in Finland, Norway and Sweden, prepared by sautéing reindeer meat. The dish originates from the Arctic Lapland where the semi-nomadic, indigenous people called Sámi have been herding reindeers for a very long time.

×

Poronkäristys Recipe

Ingredients

  • 500g thinly sliced reindeer meat (or beef as a substitute)
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 cups water
  • Salt to taste
  • Lingonberry sauce (for serving)
  • Mashed potatoes (for serving)

Prepare Reindeer Meat

  1. If the reindeer meat is not already thinly sliced, use a sharp knife to cut it into thin slices.

Sauté Onion

  1. In a large skillet or pan, melt butter over medium heat.
  2. Add thinly sliced onions and sauté until they become golden brown.

Add Reindeer Meat

  1. Add the sliced reindeer meat to the skillet.
  2. Sauté the meat until it is browned on both sides.

Add Water

  1. Pour in 2 cups of water to the skillet.
  2. Season with salt to taste.

Simmer

  1. Reduce the heat to low and let the Poronkäristys simmer for about 1.5 to 2 hours until the meat is tender.

Serve

  1. Poronkäristys is traditionally served with mashed potatoes.
  2. Plate the sliced reindeer meat on top of the mashed potatoes.
  3. Pour some of the cooking liquid and sautéed onions over the meat.
  4. Serve with lingonberry sauce on the side.

Enjoy

  1. Enjoy this traditional Finnish dish with the unique flavor of reindeer meat.

Poronkäristys is often served with a side of pickles or vegetables. Adjust the seasoning according to your taste preferences, and savor the rich flavors of this Northern dish.

Leipäjuusto

Bread cheese, known in the United States as Finnish squeaky cheese, is a Finnish fresh cheese traditionally made from cow's colostrum. In America, cow's milk is generally used but can also be made with goat milk. Commercially available versions are typically made from cow's milk.

×

Leipäjuusto Recipe

Ingredients


For the cheese

  • 1 liter of fresh milk (cow, goat, or a mix)
  • 1/4 cup buttermilk or 1 tablespoon lemon juice (for curdling)
  • A pinch of salt

For Serving

  • Fresh berries (lingonberries are traditional)
  • Honey or maple syrup

Prepare the Milk Mixture

  1. In a large pot, gently heat the fresh milk until it's lukewarm.
  2. Add buttermilk or lemon juice to the milk to initiate curdling.
  3. Stir gently and let it sit for about an hour until curds form.

Form the Curds

  1. Once curds have formed, cut them into small pieces using a knife.
  2. Gradually heat the curds while stirring constantly. Do not let it boil.

Strain and Shape

  1. Line a colander with cheesecloth or a clean kitchen towel.
  2. Pour the curds into the colander to separate the whey.
  3. Gather the cloth around the curds and shape them into a round, flat disc.

Press and Cool

  1. Place a weight (like a heavy plate) on top of the wrapped curds to press them.
  2. Let it cool and solidify for a few hours or overnight in the refrigerator.

Serve

  1. Slice the Leipäjuusto into wedges.
  2. Heat a skillet and lightly brown the cheese on both sides until it gets a golden color and a slightly crispy texture.

Serve Warm

  1. Serve the Leipäjuusto warm with fresh berries and a drizzle of honey or maple syrup.

Enjoy

  1. Savor the mild and slightly sweet flavor of Leipäjuusto with the contrasting tartness of berries and the sweetness of honey or maple syrup.

Leipäjuusto is a delightful Finnish treat, and its mild taste pairs well with various toppings and accompaniments. Adjust sweetness and serving options to suit your preferences.

    Lohikeitto

    Finnish Salmon Soup, or Lohikeitto, is a simple Nordic salmon chowder and a comforting 30 minute meal made with a light cream broth, melt-in-your-mouth chunks of salmon, and tons of fresh dill!

    ×

    Lohikeitto Recipe

    Ingredients


    For the soup

    • 500g fresh salmon fillets, cut into cubes
    • 3 medium-sized potatoes, peeled and diced
    • 1 leek, sliced
    • 2 carrots, peeled and sliced
    • 1 onion, finely chopped
    • 2 bay leaves
    • 1 liter fish or vegetable broth
    • 300ml heavy cream
    • Salt and white pepper to taste
    • 2 eggs (optional)
    • Fresh dill for garnish

    For Serving

    • Rye bread or Finnish-style bread

    Prepare Vegetables

    1. Peel and dice the potatoes.
    2. Slice the leek and carrots.
    3. Finely chop the onion.

    Cook Vegetables

    1. In a large pot, sauté the onion in a bit of oil until it becomes translucent.
    2. Add leek, carrots, and potatoes to the pot. Cook for a few minutes.

    Add Broth

    1. Pour in the fish or vegetable broth.
    2. Add bay leaves and bring the mixture to a gentle boil.
    3. Reduce heat and let it simmer until the vegetables are tender.

    Add Salmon

    1. Add the cubed salmon to the pot.
    2. Simmer for about 5-7 minutes until the salmon is cooked through.

    Finish with Cream

    1. Pour in the heavy cream.
    2. Season with salt and white pepper to taste.
    3. Simmer for an additional 5 minutes.

    Garnish and Serve

    1. Remove the bay leaves.
    2. Garnish the Lohikeitto with fresh dill.
    3. Serve the soup hot with slices of rye bread.

    Enjoy

    1. Enjoy this comforting and flavorful Finnish salmon soup!

    Lohikeitto is a delicious and hearty soup, perfect for enjoying the flavors of fresh salmon and warming up on a chilly day. Adjust seasoning and garnishes according to your preferences.

    Mykyrokka

    Mykyrokka is a soup that is a typical traditional dish in eastern Finland. The main ingredient is myky: a palm sized dumpling made from blood and barley flour. The dumplings are cooked in the soup. The soup also contains potatoes, onions, fatty meat, and offal such as kidneys, liver and or heart.

    Carnitas Recipe

    Ingredients


    For the Meat Stew

    1. 500g beef or lamb, cubed
    2. 1 large onion, finely chopped
    3. 2 carrots, peeled and sliced
    4. 2 potatoes, peeled and diced
    5. 1 turnip, peeled and diced
    6. 1 parsnip, peeled and sliced
    7. 1/2 cup pearl barley (optional)
    8. 1 liter beef or vegetable broth
    9. Salt and pepper to taste
    10. 2 bay leaves
    11. Fresh parsley for garnish

    For the Dumplings (Myky)

    1. 1 cup barley flour
    2. 1/2 cup water
    3. A pinch of salt

    Prepare Meat Stew

    1. In a large pot, brown the cubed meat over medium heat.
    2. Add chopped onions and cook until they are softened.

    Add Vegetables and Barley

    1. Add carrots, potatoes, turnip, parsnip, and pearl barley (if using) to the pot.
    2. Pour in the beef or vegetable broth.
    3. Season with salt and pepper, and add bay leaves.

    Add Aromatics

    1. Add quartered onion to the pot for added flavor.

    Simmer the Stew

    1. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to a simmer.
    2. Let it simmer for 1.5 to 2 hours, or until the meat is tender and the vegetables are cooked.

    Prepare Dumplings (Myky)

    1. In a bowl, mix barley flour, water, and a pinch of salt to form a dough.
    2. Shape the dough into small dumplings (Myky).

    Add Dumplings to Stew

    1. Drop the dumplings into the simmering stew.
    2. Cook for an additional 15-20 minutes until the dumplings are cooked through.

    Garnish and Serve

    1. Remove bay leaves from the stew.
    2. Garnish the Mykyrokka with fresh parsley.
    3. Serve hot and enjoy!

    Mykyrokka is a hearty and filling dish, perfect for colder days. Adjust the ingredients and seasoning according to your taste preferences. Serve with a side of rye bread for a complete Finnish meal.
