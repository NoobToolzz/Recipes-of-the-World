Chicken Tikka Masala

Chicken Tikka Masala is an Indian favourite along with Butter Chicken and Biryani. Serve with Basmati Rice, else add a side of chewy and fluffy Naan or basic Homemade Flatbreads to mop up the sauce!

×

Butter Chicken Recipe

Ingredients

  • 500g boneless chicken, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 3 tomatoes, pureed
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 teaspoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander for garnish

Marinate Chicken

  1. In a bowl, mix chicken with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, garam masala, chili powder, ground coriander, turmeric, cumin powder, and salt.
  2. Marinate for at least 2 hours or overnight.

Cook Chicken

  1. Preheat the oven to 200°C (400°F).
  2. Thread marinated chicken onto skewers and bake until cooked and slightly charred (about 20-25 minutes).

Prepare Masala Sauce

  1. In a pan, heat oil and sauté chopped onions until golden brown.
  2. Add tomato puree, tomato paste, and cook until the oil separates.

Spice it Up

  1. Stir in garam masala, chili powder, ground coriander, turmeric, and cumin powder.
  2. Cook for a few minutes.

Combine Chicken and Sauce

  1. Add the baked chicken pieces to the masala sauce.
  2. Pour in heavy cream and let it simmer until the chicken is fully cooked.

Garnish and Serve

  1. Garnish with fresh coriander.
  2. Serve hot with rice or naan.

Enjoy your flavorful Chicken Tikka Masala! Adjust the spice levels and creaminess to suit your taste preferences.

Biryani

Biryani is a celebration of all that is great about Indian food - the heady aromas, the vibrant colours, the fluffy rice and those addictive curry flavours. Make this Chicken Biryani with your protein of choice - or try a vegetable biryani!

×

Biryani Recipe

Ingredients

  • 2 cups basmati rice, soaked for 30 minutes
  • 500g chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 3 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/4 cup cooking oil or ghee
  • 1/4 cup chopped mint leaves
  • 1/4 cup chopped coriander leaves
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon biryani masala
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • Whole spices (bay leaves, cloves, cardamom, cinnamon)
  • Salt to taste

Marinate Chicken

  1. Mix chicken with yogurt, biryani masala, cumin powder, coriander powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Marinate for at least 1 hour.

Cook Rice

    Boil soaked basmati rice until it's 70-80% cooked. Drain the water and set aside.

Sauté Onions

  1. In a large pan, heat oil or ghee.
  2. Sauté thinly sliced onions until golden brown. Set aside a portion for garnish.

Cook Chicken

  1. In the same pan, add ginger-garlic paste and cook until fragrant.
  2. Add marinated chicken and cook until it's no longer pink.

Layer Biryani

  1. Layer the partially cooked rice over the chicken.
  2. Sprinkle chopped mint leaves, coriander leaves, and sautéed onions on top.
  3. Drizzle a bit of ghee over the layers.

Dum Cooking

  1. Cover the pan with a tight lid or aluminum foil.
  2. Cook on low heat for about 20-25 minutes to allow flavors to meld and the rice to fully cook through.

Garnish and Serve

  1. Once done, gently fluff the biryani with a fork.
  2. Garnish with the remaining sautéed onions.
  3. Serve hot with raita or yogurt.

Enjoy your delicious Chicken Biryani! Adjust spice levels according to your preference.

Butter Chicken

Butter Chicken (Murgh Makhani) is one of the most popular curries in the world and yet happens to be one of the easiest! No hunting down hard to find ingredients, this chef recipe that makes the most incredible curry sauce.

×

Butter Chicken Recipe

Ingredients

  • 500g boneless chicken, cut into cubes
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 3 tomatoes, pureed
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 teaspoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander for garnish

Marinate Chicken

  1. In a bowl, mix chicken with yogurt and let it marinate for at least 30 minutes.

Cook Chicken

  1. In a pan, melt butter and sauté chopped onions until golden brown.
  2. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook until fragrant.
  3. Add marinated chicken and cook until it turns white.

Add Tomatoes and Spices

  1. Pour in pureed tomatoes and tomato paste.
  2. Stir in garam masala, chili powder, turmeric, ground cumin, and salt.
  3. Simmer until the chicken is cooked through.

Finish with Cream

  1. Pour in heavy cream and mix well.
  2. Simmer for a few more minutes until the sauce thickens.

Garnish and Serve

  1. Garnish with fresh coriander and serve with rice or naan.

Samosa

Indulge in the allure of these flawless pyramids of flaky pastry, generously filled with a delightful spiced potato mixture! Not all samosas are born equal—some fall short with dry textures, insufficient fillings, inferior pastry, or lackluster spicing. Embark on the culinary adventure of crafting your own perfect samosas, and now, behold the impeccable Samosa recipe presented here for your enjoyment.

×

Samosa Recipe

Ingredients

  • 2 cups boiled and mashed potatoes
  • 1 cup peas (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • Samosa wrappers or pastry sheets
  • Oil for frying

Prepare Filling

  1. In a pan, heat oil and add cumin seeds.
  2. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté until fragrant.
  3. Mix in mashed potatoes, peas (if using), and spices.
  4. Cook until well combined and aromatic.
  5. Stir in fresh coriander leaves and let the filling cool.

Assemble Samosas

  1. Take samosa wrappers or cut pastry sheets into triangles.
  2. Form a cone shape and fill with the prepared potato mixture.
  3. Seal the edges with water to form a triangular pocket.

Fry Samosas

  1. Heat oil in a pan for deep frying.
  2. Fry samosas until golden brown and crispy.

Drain and Serve

  1. Remove samosas and place them on paper towels to drain excess oil.
  2. Serve hot with mint chutney or tamarind sauce.

Enjoy your homemade Samosas! Feel free to customize the filling and adjust spices according to your taste preferences.
