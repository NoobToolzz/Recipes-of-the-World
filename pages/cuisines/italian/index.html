Pizza

Pizza is a dish of Italian origin consisting of a usually round, flat base of leavened wheat-based dough topped with tomatoes, cheese, and often various other ingredients, which is then baked at a high temperature, traditionally in a wood-fired oven.

×

Pizza Recipe

Ingredients


For the Dough

  • 2 1/4 teaspoons (1 packet) active dry yeast
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 3/4 cup warm water (around 110°F/43°C)
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil

For the Pizza Sauce

  • 1 can (14 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Toppings

  • Shredded mozzarella cheese
  • Your favorite pizza toppings (e.g., pepperoni, bell peppers, mushrooms, olives)

Mix Dry Ingredients

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine active dry yeast, sugar, and warm water. Let it sit for 5-10 minutes until frothy.

Prepare Pizza Dough

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine flour and salt. Make a well in the center and add the yeast mixture and olive oil.
  2. Mix until a dough forms. Knead on a floured surface for about 5-7 minutes until smooth.
  3. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover with a damp cloth, and let it rise for 1-1.5 hours or until doubled in size.

Preheat Oven

  1. Preheat your oven to the highest temperature it can go (usually around 475°F/245°C).

Prepare Pizza Sauce

  1. Mix crushed tomatoes, minced garlic, oregano, basil, salt, and pepper in a bowl. Set aside.

Roll Out Dough and Assemble Pizza

  1. Roll out the dough on a floured surface and place it on a pizza stone or baking sheet.
  2. Spread pizza sauce over the dough and add shredded mozzarella and your favorite toppings.

Bake

  1. Bake in the preheated oven for 12-15 minutes, or until crust is golden and cheese is melted.

Garnish and Serve

  1. Garnish with fresh basil leaves, grated Parmesan, and red pepper flakes if desired.
  2. Slice and enjoy your homemade pizza!

Feel free to customize your pizza with various toppings and experiment with different cheeses and sauces. Enjoy your homemade pizza straight from the oven!

Arancini

Arancini, also arancine, are Italian rice balls that are stuffed, coated with breadcrumbs and deep-fried. They are a staple of Sicilian cuisine.

×

Arancini Recipe

Ingredients


For the Risotto

  • 1 cup Arborio rice
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable broth, heated
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1/2 cup diced mozzarella cheese

For the Arancini Coating

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs, beaten
  • 1 cup fine breadcrumbs
  • Vegetable oil for frying

For the Filling (optional)

  • Additional diced mozzarella cheese
  • Peas, cooked
  • Bolognese sauce

Prepare Risotto

  1. In a large pan, toast Arborio rice over medium heat for a few minutes.
  2. Deglaze with white wine and cook until absorbed.
  3. Gradually add warm broth, stirring frequently until rice is creamy and cooked al dente.
  4. Stir in Parmesan, salt, pepper, butter, and diced mozzarella. Allow to cool.

Form Arancini Balls

  1. Take a scoop of risotto and flatten it in your hand.
  2. Place a small amount of optional filling in the center.
  3. Enclose the filling, shaping the mixture into golf ball-sized arancini.

Coat and Fry

  1. Roll each arancini in flour, dip in beaten eggs, and coat with breadcrumbs.
  2. Heat vegetable oil in a pan and fry arancini until golden brown on all sides.
  3. Drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.

Serve

  1. Serve arancini hot, optionally with marinara or tomato sauce for dipping.

Arancini are perfect as appetizers or snacks. Get creative with different fillings or enjoy them as they are!

Focaccia

Focaccia is a flat leavened oven-baked Italian bread. In some contemporary places, such as Rome, it is a style of pizza, also called pizza bianca. Focaccia may be served as a side dish or as sandwich bread and it may be round, rectangular, or square shape.

×

Focaccia Recipe

Ingredients


For the Dough

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons salt
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon active dry yeast
  • 1 1/2 cups warm water (110°F/43°C)
  • 1/4 cup olive oil (plus extra for drizzling)

For Topping (Optional)

  • Cherry tomatoes, halved
  • Fresh rosemary
  • Olives, pitted and sliced
  • Sea salt flakes
  • Parmesan cheese, grated

Prepare Dough

  1. In a bowl, mix warm water, sugar, and active dry yeast. Let it sit for 5-10 minutes until frothy.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine flour and salt. Add yeast mixture and olive oil.
  3. Knead the dough on a floured surface for 8-10 minutes until smooth and elastic.
  4. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover, and let it rise for 1-2 hours or until doubled in size.

Shape and Second Rise

  1. Punch down the risen dough and transfer it to a baking sheet.
  2. Press the dough into a rectangle or desired shape on the baking sheet.
  3. Cover and let it rise for an additional 30-45 minutes.

Preheat and Top

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. If desired, press halved cherry tomatoes, fresh rosemary, olives, sea salt, and grated Parmesan onto the surface of the dough.
  3. Drizzle with olive oil.

Bake

  1. Bake in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes or until golden brown and cooked through.

Cool and Serve

  1. Allow the focaccia to cool on a wire rack before slicing.
  2. Serve and enjoy your homemade focaccia!

Focaccia is a versatile bread that pairs well with dips, cheeses, or makes a delicious sandwich. Experiment with toppings to suit your taste.

Lasagna

One of the most loved foods in the world is finally here! This is a beautiful Italian Lasagna with layers of slow cooked Ragù Bolognese and Besciamella cheese sauce. Though patience is required, it is quite straight forward to make as you will see in the recipe video!

×

Lasagna Recipe

Ingredients


For the Meat Sauce

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (28 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 can (14 oz) tomato sauce
  • 1 can (6 oz) tomato paste
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 2 teaspoons dried basil
  • 2 teaspoons dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper

For the Ricotta Filling

  • 2 cups ricotta cheese
  • 1 egg
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper

Additional Layers

  • Lasagna noodles, cooked
  • 3 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese

Prepare Meat Sauce

  1. In a large pot, cook ground beef over medium heat until browned. Drain excess fat.
  2. Add chopped onion and minced garlic, cook until softened.
  3. Stir in crushed tomatoes, tomato sauce, tomato paste, water, basil, oregano, salt, and pepper. Simmer for 30 minutes.

Prepare Ricotta Filling

  1. In a bowl, combine ricotta cheese, egg, chopped parsley, salt, and pepper. Mix well.

Assemble Lasagna

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a baking dish, spread a layer of meat sauce.
  3. Place a layer of cooked lasagna noodles on top.
  4. Spread half of the ricotta filling over the noodles.
  5. Sprinkle with mozzarella and Parmesan cheese.
  6. Repeat the layers, ending with a layer of meat sauce and a generous amount of cheese on top.

Bake

  1. Cover with foil and bake for 25 minutes. Remove foil and bake for an additional 25 minutes or until bubbly and golden.

Cool and Serve

  1. Allow lasagna to cool for 15 minutes before slicing.
  2. Serve and enjoy your classic homemade lasagna!

Lasagna is a comforting and hearty dish that's perfect for gatherings. Customize it with your favorite ingredients and enjoy the layers of flavor.

Osso Buco

Comfort food central! Fall apart tender veal shanks bathed in a tomato white wine sauce, Osso Buco is a traditional Italian dish that's both hearty and luxurious owing to the delicate flavour and texture of veal.

×

Osso Buco Recipe

Ingredients


For the Osso Buco

  • 4 veal shanks, about 1 1/2 inches thick
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour, for dredging
  • 4 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 carrots, peeled and finely chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup dry white wine
  • 1 can (28 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 cup chicken or beef broth
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)
  • Grated lemon zest (optional, for garnish)

Dredge and Brown Veal

  1. Season veal shanks with salt and pepper, then dredge in flour, shaking off excess.
  2. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium-high heat. Brown veal on all sides. Remove and set aside.

Sauté Vegetables

  1. In the same skillet, sauté chopped onion, carrots, celery, and garlic until softened.

Deglaze and Simmer

  1. Pour in white wine, scraping up any browned bits. Add crushed tomatoes, broth, bay leaf, thyme, and rosemary.
  2. Return veal to the skillet, bring to a simmer, then cover and let it simmer for 2 hours or until veal is tender.

Garnish and Serve

  1. Remove bay leaf. Garnish with chopped parsley and lemon zest if desired.
  2. Serve Osso Buco over a bed of risotto, mashed potatoes, or polenta.

Osso Buco is a classic Italian dish that combines the richness of veal shanks with a flavorful tomato-based sauce. Enjoy this hearty meal with your favorite accompaniment.
